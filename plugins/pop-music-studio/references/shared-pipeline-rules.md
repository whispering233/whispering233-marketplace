# Shared Pipeline Rules

These rules apply to every output produced through `pop-music-studio`, including lyrics, song composition, arrangement, vocals, cover art, and MV planning.

## 1. One Brief, One Source of Truth

Every stage must explicitly restate:

- project title or working title
- release goal
- artist persona
- target audience
- language
- emotional target

Do not let later stages silently drift away from the original brief.

## 2. Stage Inputs, Outputs, and Handoffs

Every stage output must make these explicit:

- input materials it depends on
- output artifacts it produces
- what the next stage should inherit

If a stage is missing upstream context, mark it as `To be confirmed` instead of pretending the dependency is already fixed.

## 3. Pop Music Guardrails

When the request is for commercial or mainstream pop music, keep these constraints visible when relevant:

- hook clarity
- singable line length
- memorable section contrast
- a recognizable emotional arc
- audience-aware language and imagery
- repeatable visual motifs for release packaging

Do not optimize only for poetic writing if the result becomes difficult to sing, arrange, or market.

## 4. Cross-Stage Consistency

Lyrics, music, vocals, cover art, and MV direction should align on:

- core theme
- mood words
- era or sonic references
- recurring imagery
- character or narrator identity
- energy level from section to section

If a later stage intentionally breaks consistency, explain why as a deliberate creative decision.

## 5. Explicit Creative Decisions

Record decisions in structured form, not hidden in loose prose.

Examples:

- title choice and why it wins
- BPM, key, or groove direction and why it fits
- instrumentation priorities
- vocal timbre and performance intent
- cover palette and typography direction
- MV concept, scene type, and edit rhythm

## 6. Output Format

Required output style:

- structured headings
- stable, scannable stage sections
- tables for compact brief data
- short explanatory paragraphs where tradeoffs matter
- dedicated sections for risks and open questions

Do not deliver stage outputs as:

- pure unstructured brainstorming
- lyrics with no brief or hook rationale
- arrangement notes with no section mapping
- visual prompts with no concept explanation

## 7. Risks and Open Questions

Every stage must include:

- assumptions
- known risks
- open questions
- revision or next-step notes

If information is missing, keep the section and mark it as `To be confirmed`.
