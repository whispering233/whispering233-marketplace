# Lyrics Writing

## Overview

Use this skill for structured lyric-writing outputs.

Read these files in order:

1. `../../references/shared-pipeline-rules.md`
2. `../../templates/lyrics-template.md`

## Required Workflow

1. Clarify the project brief, artist persona, target audience, language, and emotional target.
2. Lock the song point of view, title direction, and hook phrase before drafting the full lyric.
3. Write the lyric in explicit song sections instead of free-form prose.
4. Preserve singability, repetition, and hook clarity.
5. Keep alternative lines, risks, and open questions visible.

## Required Content

The final output must cover, when relevant:

- stage brief
- concept and imagery direction
- title or hook candidates
- full lyric draft by section
- alternate chorus or hook options
- rhyme, cadence, and singability notes
- sensitive-content notes
- revision notes and open questions

## Quality Bar

- do not output a poem with no song structure
- do not list only themes without a drafted lyric
- do not make lines so dense that they are hard to sing
- do not hide missing decisions; mark them as `To be confirmed`

## Common Failure Modes

Avoid these outputs:

- a mood description with no usable lyric sections
- a catchy hook with no supporting verses
- polished poetry that does not match the intended audience or style
- lyrics that ignore the later melody and arrangement handoff
