# Vocal Performance

## Overview

Use this skill for structured vocal-performance outputs.

Read these files in order:

1. `../../references/shared-pipeline-rules.md`
2. `../../templates/vocal-template.md`

## Required Workflow

1. Restate the song brief, singer persona, and emotional target.
2. Define the lead-vocal identity before section-level direction.
3. Describe delivery, register, articulation, and dynamic choices by section.
4. Specify harmony, doubles, ad-libs, and processing notes.
5. Preserve cover-art and MV handoff notes, risks, and open questions.

## Required Content

The final output must cover, when relevant:

- stage brief
- lead-vocal identity
- section-by-section delivery notes
- harmony and double strategy
- ad-lib moments
- recording priorities
- processing intent
- visual persona cues for downstream artwork and MV work
- risks and unresolved questions

## Quality Bar

- do not reduce the stage to generic adjectives only
- do not ignore section contrast between verses, pre-chorus, and chorus
- do not separate vocal direction from the song and arrangement context
- do not hide missing decisions; mark them as `To be confirmed`

## Common Failure Modes

Avoid these outputs:

- a singer moodboard with no performance instructions
- harmony notes with no lead-vocal anchor
- ad-libs that clutter the hook instead of supporting it
- vocal notes that break the artist persona defined upstream
