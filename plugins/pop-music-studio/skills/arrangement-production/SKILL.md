# Arrangement Production

## Overview

Use this skill for structured arrangement and production outputs.

Read these files in order:

1. `../../references/shared-pipeline-rules.md`
2. `../../templates/arrangement-template.md`

## Required Workflow

1. Restate the song brief, sonic references, and section map.
2. Define the production palette before writing section-level arrangement notes.
3. Specify instrumentation, rhythmic feel, and energy movement for each section.
4. Record transitions, effects, automation ideas, and standout moments.
5. Preserve vocal handoff notes, risks, and revision priorities.

## Required Content

The final output must cover, when relevant:

- stage brief
- production palette and references
- instrumentation priorities
- section-by-section arrangement notes
- rhythm, groove, and texture strategy
- transitions, drops, and lift moments
- vocal-support notes
- mix-focus and handoff notes
- risks and open questions

## Quality Bar

- do not output only a list of instruments
- do not skip section-level changes in energy or texture
- do not ignore how the arrangement supports the topline and hook
- do not hide unknown decisions; mark them as `To be confirmed`

## Common Failure Modes

Avoid these outputs:

- a genre label with no practical arrangement plan
- instrumentation notes with no section map
- loudness or density that fights the vocal at the hook
- arrangement directions that do not hand off cleanly to vocal production
