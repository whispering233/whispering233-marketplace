# Song Composition

## Overview

Use this skill for structured song-composition outputs.

Read these files in order:

1. `../../references/shared-pipeline-rules.md`
2. `../../templates/song-template.md`

## Required Workflow

1. Restate the lyric brief, release target, and emotional arc.
2. Define song architecture before expanding section details.
3. Specify BPM, key, mode, duration target, and section energy.
4. Describe topline, hook, and chord direction in a way that a producer or songwriter can act on.
5. Preserve arrangement handoff notes, risks, and open questions.

## Required Content

The final output must cover, when relevant:

- stage brief
- sonic references
- BPM, key, mode, and duration target
- section map and energy curve
- topline and hook direction by section
- chord or harmonic guidance
- standout motif or signature moment
- handoff notes for arrangement
- risks and unresolved questions

## Quality Bar

- do not write only abstract mood words
- do not skip structure, tempo, or key decisions when they matter
- do not describe melody in a way that cannot be turned into production notes
- do not hide uncertainty; mark it as `To be confirmed`

## Common Failure Modes

Avoid these outputs:

- a song summary with no section-level detail
- chord names with no melodic or dynamic context
- a catchy chorus concept with no verse-to-chorus contrast plan
- composition notes that cannot hand off cleanly to arrangement
